# HomeHandyman Business Model

## Overview

HomeHandyman operates a **two-sided marketplace** connecting Singapore homeowners with vetted handymen/contractors. Our business model is designed to be fair, transparent, and scalable.

---

## Revenue Model

### Platform Fee Structure

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Platform Fee** | 20% of total job value |
| **Handyman Earnings** | 80% of total job value |
| **Customer Pays** | Service fee + 20% platform fee |

### Example Breakdown

| Job Value | Customer Pays | Handyman Receives | HomeHandyman Earns |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| $50 | $60 | $40 | $10 |
| $100 | $120 | $80 | $20 |
| $200 | $240 | $160 | $40 |
| $500 | $600 | $400 | $100 |

## Value Proposition

### For Customers
- ✅ Access to vetted, background-checked handymen
- ✅ Transparent pricing (no hidden fees)
- ✅ Insurance coverage on all jobs
- ✅ 30-minute matching guarantee
- ✅ Quality guarantee (fix or refund)

### For Handymen
- ✅ **Free to join** — no subscription or signup fees
- ✅ Keep 80% of earnings (industry-leading)
- ✅ Steady stream of qualified leads
- ✅ Insurance coverage provided
- ✅ Build reputation through ratings/reviews
- ✅ Flexible schedule — accept jobs when available

## Growth Strategy

### Phase 1: Launch (Months 1-3)
- Target: 50-100 jobs/month
- Focus: Build handyman network, refine operations
- Marketing: Word-of-mouth, local Facebook groups, HDB forums

### Phase 2: Growth (Months 4-12)
- Target: 200-300 jobs/month
- Focus: Scale marketing, improve matching algorithm
- Marketing: Google Ads, SEO, partnerships with property agents

### Phase 3: Scale (Year 2+)
- Target: 500+ jobs/month
- Focus: Expand services, geographic coverage
- Marketing: Brand campaigns, referral programs, corporate partnerships

## Risk Factors

1. **Supply Risk**: Not enough qualified handymen
   - Mitigation: Aggressive recruitment, referral bonuses

2. **Demand Risk**: Not enough customers
   - Mitigation: Marketing investment, promotional pricing

3. **Quality Risk**: Poor service quality damages brand
   - Mitigation: Strict vetting, insurance, guarantee program

4. **Competition**: New entrants or incumbent response
   - Mitigation: Build brand loyalty, network effects
